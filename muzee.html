Museum of Art

Museum of Art from Timisoara - Baroque Palace is one of the most valuable historical monuments of Timisoara. With the year 1751, when the separation of the military and the military administration began in Banat, the military engineers became preoccupied with finding a space inside the fortress for the residence of the governor's house. Moreover, the appearance of the building is eulogiously mentioned in the work of Francisc Griselini, which appeared in Bucharest in 1926. The building still preserves this form today. The new name of the monument is The President's Palace. The President's Palace appears as one of the most representative constructions in Timisoara in the 18th century and confirms the administration's preoccupation weather for the beauty of the city..

The Museum of the Metropolitan Cathedral

The Old Church Art Collection of the Timisoara Archdiocese located in the basement of the Metropolitan Cathedral in Timisoara. The heritage of church art now houses nearly 1,000 pieces, including a significant number of wood icons, glass and cloth, embroidered, carved or metalwork, and an important religious book fund. They all come from the Banat churches and date back to the 14th, 16th, 17th and 19th centuries. Documents of undeniable historical significance, they express the cultural level in Banat, while emphasizing the sensitivity for the beauty of the faithful..

Communist Consumer Museum

Arranged in the form of a "regular" dwelling in the Golden Age, with living room, children's room, kitchen, closet, bathroom, etc. The Communist Consumer Museum includes "everything Romanians have at home": domestic and decorative items of Romanian production, furniture, smuggled goods, handmade objects of the time, clothing and clothing accessories, footwear, household articles, home appliances, games, toys, audio tapes, vinyl records, cosmetics, stationery. It is intended for tourists, children, grandparents. "Before 1989, we were surprised at what they had, now wondering why we were," says the initiator of the Communist Consumer Museum, Ovidiu Mihăiţă, the artist who founded the first independent theater 10 years ago in Timisoara, Aualeu Theater.
